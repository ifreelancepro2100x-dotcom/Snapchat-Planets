What Are Snapchat Planets?

Snapchat Planets are part of the Snapchat Plus subscription. This premium feature uses our solar system to show where you rank among a friend's Best Friends.

Think of yourself as a planet orbiting your friend, who represents the Sun. The closer your planet sits to the Sun, the stronger your friendship. There are eight planets in total, and each one corresponds to a specific Best Friend ranking.

Unlike public follower counts or visible friend lists on other platforms, Snapchat Planets give you a private, playful way to visualize how much you interact with the people you talk to most.

How Does the Solar System Work?

Your position is based on your recent interactions with another user, including:

  • Sending and receiving Snaps
  • Chat conversations
  • Replying to Stories
  • Sharing content
  • Maintaining Snapstreaks
  • Overall communication frequency

The more consistently you interact, the closer your planet moves toward the Sun. If you're someone's Mercury, you're their closest Best Friend. Shift to Venus or Earth and it usually means another friend has become more active lately.

Remember: these rankings are dynamic. They change as communication patterns change over time.

Do You Need Snapchat Plus?

Yes. Snapchat Planets are available exclusively through Snapchat Plus, the platform's premium subscription.

Without it, you can still have Best Friends, keep Snapstreaks, and enjoy the standard experience — but you won't see the Solar System or your planetary ranking. The Friend Solar System is just one of several exclusive perks that make Snapchat Plus popular with users who love extra personalization.

Snapchat Planets in Order

The Solar System follows the same order as the real one. Each planet represents a different friendship rank — here are the first four positions.

Mercury

#1 Best Friend

The closest planet to the Sun — the highest ranking. You're their number one, exchanging Snaps and chatting more than anyone else.

Venus

#2 Best Friend

The second closest friendship. Still one of their strongest connections, but someone else edges you out slightly on recent activity.

Earth

#3 Best Friend

Consistently active together. You trade messages and Snaps regularly, just a touch less than Mercury and Venus.

Mars

#4 Best Friend

A solid, active friendship. You're among their closest contacts — regular chats and Snap exchanges keep this spot secure.
